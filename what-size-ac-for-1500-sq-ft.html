What Size AC for a 1,500 Sq Ft House?

The Short Answer: 2 to 2.5 Tons

A 1,500 square foot house needs an air conditioner rated between 2 and 2.5 tons (24,000 to 30,000 BTU/hr). This is one of the most common home sizes in the United States, and it falls right in the sweet spot where getting the tonnage exactly right makes a noticeable difference in comfort and energy bills.

At 1,500 sq ft, most homes have three bedrooms and a living area connected by a hallway. The challenge is getting cool air distributed evenly to every room, especially back bedrooms that sit at the end of long duct runs. An oversized system blasts cold air and shuts off before those far rooms get comfortable. A right-sized system runs longer cycles that push conditioned air throughout the entire house.

Why 1,500 Sq Ft Is the Tipping Point

Two-Story Consideration

Many 1,500 sq ft homes are two stories. The upstairs is always warmer. A properly sized system with balanced ductwork handles this. An oversized unit makes it worse by cooling the downstairs too fast.

The 3-Bedroom Standard

Most 1,500 sq ft homes have 3 bedrooms. Each occupied bedroom adds body heat at night when the AC runs less. Account for occupancy when choosing between 2 and 2.5 tons.

Ductwork Matters More

At this size, duct layout significantly affects performance. Leaky or undersized ducts can waste 25-40% of cooling capacity, making a right-sized system seem too small.

Energy Bill Impact

The difference between a 2 ton and 3 ton unit running all summer is $150-$300 in annual electricity costs. Right-sizing pays for itself quickly.

When to Go with 2.5 Tons

  • Sun Belt location — If your outdoor design temperature exceeds 95°F, the extra capacity handles peak cooling days
  • Older construction (pre-1990) — Homes built before modern energy codes often have R-11 walls and minimal attic insulation
  • Lots of windows — Especially bay windows, sliding glass doors, or floor-to-ceiling glass on the south or west side
  • Dark roof — Dark shingles absorb more solar heat, increasing attic temperatures and the cooling load on rooms below
  • Open kitchen — Cooking produces significant heat, and an open-concept kitchen shares that load with the living space

When 2 Tons Is Enough

  • Built after 2000 — Modern energy codes require R-13+ walls and R-30+ attic insulation, reducing the cooling load substantially
  • Low-E double-pane windows — These reduce solar heat gain by up to 70% compared to clear single-pane glass
  • Shade trees on south/west — Mature shade trees can reduce cooling loads by 20-30%
  • Northern climate — Shorter cooling seasons mean the AC doesn't need to handle as many extreme-heat hours
  • Sealed and tested ductwork — Tight ducts deliver the full rated capacity to each room

Single-Stage vs. Two-Stage for 1,500 Sq Ft

If your budget allows, a two-stage or variable-speed AC is especially beneficial at the 1,500 sq ft size. A two-stage compressor runs at low capacity (about 60-70%) most of the time and shifts to full capacity only on the hottest days. This means a 2.5 ton two-stage unit effectively operates as a 1.5-1.75 ton system during normal conditions, providing the long run times that maximize comfort and dehumidification, while still having full 2.5 ton capacity when you need it.

For a single-stage system, sizing accuracy matters more because the unit is always at 100% capacity or off. A 2.5 ton single-stage in a mild climate will short-cycle more than a 2 ton unit would. But a 2.5 ton two-stage avoids this problem entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size AC do I need for a 1,500 sq ft house?

A 1,500 square foot house typically needs a 2 to 2.5 ton air conditioner (24,000-30,000 BTU). Homes in hot climates like Texas or Florida should go with 2.5 tons, while homes in mild or cold climates can use a 2 ton system.

How many BTU do I need for 1,500 square feet?

You need 24,000 to 30,000 BTU for a 1,500 sq ft home. The range accounts for differences in climate, insulation, window area, and building age. A newer home in a temperate climate needs closer to 24,000 BTU, while an older home in the Sun Belt may need 30,000 BTU.

Is a 2.5 ton AC enough for 1,500 sq ft?

Yes, a 2.5 ton AC (30,000 BTU) is typically the upper end of what a 1,500 sq ft home needs. It provides adequate capacity for hot climates and homes with higher heat loads from poor insulation or lots of windows. In mild climates, 2 tons is often the better choice to avoid short-cycling.

Can a 2 ton AC cool a 1,500 sq ft house in Texas?

A 2 ton AC can cool a 1,500 sq ft house in Texas if the home is well-insulated with modern windows and good air sealing. However, most Texas homes are better served by a 2.5 ton unit given the sustained 100°F+ summer temperatures and humidity in coastal regions. What does it cost to install AC in a 1,500 sq ft house?

Installing a central AC system in a 1,500 sq ft house costs $3,500-$7,000 on average, depending on whether ductwork exists. A 2-2.5 ton unit costs less than a 3 ton unit, so right-sizing saves money upfront in addition to lowering monthly energy bills.
