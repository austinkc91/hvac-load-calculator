What Size AC for a 1,000 Sq Ft House?

A 1,000 square foot home needs a 1.5 to 2 ton air conditioner (18,000-24,000 BTU). Get the exact size for your climate zone below.

The Short Answer: 1.5 to 2 Tons

For a 1,000 square foot house, you need an air conditioner rated between 1.5 and 2 tons (18,000 to 24,000 BTU/hr). The exact size depends on where you live, how well your home is insulated, and how many windows face south or west.

A 1,000 sq ft home is on the smaller side for central air, which means getting the size right is especially important. An oversized unit in a compact house will short-cycle aggressively, creating cold spots near the vents and muggy air everywhere else. A right-sized system runs long, steady cycles that keep humidity under control and energy bills low.

What Makes a 1,000 Sq Ft Home Different

Smaller Duct Systems

With fewer rooms and shorter duct runs, airflow is easier to balance. But undersized or poorly sealed ducts can still waste 20-30% of your cooling capacity.

Faster Temperature Changes

A smaller home heats up and cools down faster. A right-sized AC responds quickly without wasting energy cycling on and off every few minutes.

Common Floor Plans

Starter homes, condos, and ranch-style houses often fall in this range. Open floor plans in this size are ideal for a single-zone system.

Lower Installation Cost

A 1.5-2 ton system costs $3,000-$5,500 installed on average. Avoid paying for a 2.5 or 3 ton unit you don't need.

Factors That Push You to 2 Tons

Even within a 1,000 sq ft home, certain conditions mean you should go with the larger 2-ton system:

  • Poor insulation — Older homes built before 1980 often have minimal wall insulation and single-pane windows
  • Large west-facing windows — Afternoon sun pours heat directly into the home during peak cooling hours
  • Cathedral or vaulted ceilings — Higher ceilings mean more air volume to cool, even at the same square footage
  • Kitchen-heavy layout — If the kitchen is a large portion of the floor plan, cooking appliances add significant internal heat
  • Hot climate — Outdoor design temperatures above 95°F consistently push the load higher

Factors That Keep You at 1.5 Tons

  • New construction — Modern building codes require better insulation and tighter air sealing
  • Double-pane or Low-E windows — These cut solar heat gain by 40-70% compared to single-pane
  • Tree shade — Mature trees on the south and west sides dramatically reduce cooling load
  • Northern or mild climate — Shorter cooling seasons and lower peak temperatures
  • Small household — One or two occupants generate less internal heat than a family of four

Mini-Split vs. Central Air for 1,000 Sq Ft

At 1,000 square feet, you have a real choice between a traditional central air system and a ductless mini-split. A single-zone mini-split in the 18,000-24,000 BTU range can cool an open-concept 1,000 sq ft home very effectively, often at a lower installation cost than central air if ductwork doesn't already exist.

If your home has existing ductwork in good condition, central air makes sense. If you're adding AC to a home that's never had it — such as an older bungalow or a converted space — a mini-split avoids the cost and disruption of installing ducts. For homes with separate bedrooms that need independent temperature control, a multi-zone mini-split with two indoor heads gives you room-by-room comfort.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size AC do I need for a 1,000 sq ft house?

A 1,000 square foot home typically needs a 1.5 to 2 ton air conditioner (18,000-24,000 BTU). In hot climates like Arizona or Texas, go with 2 tons. In mild or cold climates, 1.5 tons is usually sufficient.

How many BTU do I need for 1,000 square feet?

You need 18,000 to 24,000 BTU for a 1,000 sq ft home. The exact number depends on your climate zone, insulation quality, window count, and ceiling height. A well-insulated home in a mild climate needs closer to 18,000 BTU, while a home in a hot, humid climate may need the full 24,000 BTU.

Can a 1.5 ton AC cool a 1,000 sq ft house?

Yes, a 1.5 ton AC (18,000 BTU) can effectively cool a 1,000 sq ft house if it is well-insulated, located in a mild to moderate climate, and does not have excessive south or west-facing windows. In hot climates or older homes with poor insulation, a 2 ton unit is the better choice.

Is a 2 ton AC too big for 1,000 sq ft?

A 2 ton AC is not too big for 1,000 sq ft in hot or humid climates. However, in cooler regions or very well-insulated homes, a 2 ton unit may short-cycle, meaning it turns on and off too frequently. This reduces humidity control and increases wear on the compressor. In those cases, 1.5 tons is the better fit.

What is the monthly electric cost to cool a 1,000 sq ft house?

A properly sized AC for a 1,000 sq ft house typically costs $60-$120 per month in cooling season, depending on your electricity rate and climate. A right-sized unit runs efficient cycles and avoids the energy waste caused by oversized systems that short-cycle or undersized systems that run nonstop.
